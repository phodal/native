improvement: **Linux hidden-titlebar fidelity**: `.hidden_inset`/`.hidden_inset_tall` windows on the GTK host now come up with real client-side decorations instead of a bare undecorated window — a header bar carrying the desktop-themed close/minimize/maximize controls (side and order follow the user's `gtk-decoration-layout` setting, live), with the tall style floored at the same 52pt band as macOS. The window-chrome channel reports the band height plus a leading or trailing controls inset per the user's layout, and markup `window-drag` regions are consumed at the press gesture like a real titlebar: system move, the user's double-click convention, and press-claiming widgets inside a region keep their press.
