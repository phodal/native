feature: **Charts in Native markup**: `<chart>` is a first-class markup composite — `<series kind="area" values="{cpuHistory}" color="accent"/>` children bind model `[]const f32` iterables through the same resolution set as `for each` (fields, pub decls, fns — arena fns and slice-valued template args included), and both engines lower it through `ui.chart`, so markup charts are pixel- and semantics-identical to Zig-built ones (parity-suite proven). Kinds are `line`/`area`/`bar`; `y-min`/`y-max`/`grid-lines`/`baseline`/`stroke-width` mirror the builder options; `NaN` samples draw nothing (the padding idiom lives in a model fn).
- Total across the stack: validator and both engines reject misuse with teaching errors (a non-f32 `values` binding, `kind="band"` — which needs a paired lower edge and stays with `ui.chart` — dynamic series composition, handlers on the display-only chart), `native check` types series bindings against the model contract and names the actual item type in the finding, NSUI serializes the new vocabulary under fresh registry codes (additive — no schema-version bump), and the LSP/docs vocabulary gains chart/series completion, hover, and attribute tables.
- The system-monitor sparklines (three `spark_*.native` fragments) and deck's spectrum analyzer (`spectrum.native`) moved from Zig chart views to markup charts, pixel-identical by the deterministic screenshot renderers.
